Grass

Native Wallaby Grass seen in the Grass Circle can be described as long, thin blades, with small seeds at the end of their stems.



Below, there is a thick layer of grey, dead grass, obscuring the dirt underneath. If you were to sit on them, you would hardly get any dirt onto you.



In stark contrast to the Grass Circle, another area nearby features heavily trimmed down turf, a clear sign of constant human activity. Here, the cracked soil is visible underneath, appearing as though ready to give out at a moment's notice.

Here can also be found various species of birds, notably magpies and noisy miners. At certain times of the year, magpies will flock to the grasslands to graze on bugs.
